The 3 mistakes that ruin good wine

Temperature is the biggest mistake. Too warm, and wine tastes boozy. Too cold, and you miss the complexity. Reds should be slightly cooler than room temperature—if your house is warm, 15 minutes in the fridge before opening. Whites should be fridge-cold.

The second mistake: storing wine near heat or light. Find an interior cabinet that stays cool year-round.

The third: letting open wine go to waste. A vacuum pump removes air; the fridge slows oxidation. Even reds go in the fridge once opened—just let them warm up before the next pour.
