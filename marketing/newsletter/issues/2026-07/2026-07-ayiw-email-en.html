The grapes are changing color. Here's what July looks like in the vineyard and cellar.

Making Wine — Veraison

It's midsummer, the grapes on the vines are filling in size and gradually becoming the color they will be when they're ready for harvest. The change of color is called 'veraison'. Everything is moving quickly as we prepare for harvest.

We make yield assessments based on the clusters per vine ratio, and by the end of July we will be regularly sampling the grapes in the vineyard in order to monitor the pace and health of the ripening process. The sampling is an intense task. In order to reach an "average" of the vineyard, individual grapes are picked from different clusters all around each plot.

The cellar is quieter, but not idle. We're checking tanks, testing pumps, and getting everything ready for the most intense season of the year, the harvest. In between, we're finishing the blending decisions on last year's wines: deciding what stands on its own, and what needs a partner.

Harvest is close. You can feel the excitement in the air.
