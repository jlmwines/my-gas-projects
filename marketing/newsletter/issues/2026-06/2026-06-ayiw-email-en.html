The grapes are forming. Here's what June looks like in the vineyard and cellar.

Making Wine — Fruit Set

June is when the vineyard first gives answers. The vines' flowers have turned into actual grapes — small, green, and hard. This stage is called Fruit Set, and it lets us estimate the coming harvest for the first time.

If the yield looks too generous, we'll thin the bunches — removing clusters so the rest can concentrate their flavors. The canopy work continues too: guiding new shoots into the trellis wires to keep the air moving and the grapes healthy.

In the cellar, the pace is quieter. We top the oak barrels as wine slowly evaporates, and taste often. Some wines are already showing bright, expressive flavors. Others are still closed and reserved. Both can become something excellent.
