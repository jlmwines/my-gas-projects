What's happening in the vineyard this month — a note from Evyatar.

Out in the Vineyard

A lot of people picture winemaking as something that happens in the cellar, but the truth is most of what decides this year's wine is happening right now, out in the vineyard.

It's late May, and the vines are running. Shoots that were tight little buds a few weeks ago are now waist-high and continuing to grow. On the vines, the first clusters are forming: pinhead-sized, hard, packed in tight. Already at this point, some need to be removed. It sounds counterintuitive, but removing extra fruit early is how the rest gets enough of what the vine has to give.

The winery is quieter this month. We taste from the barrels periodically to see where last year's wine is heading. The bottling line is finishing the current release, and once those cases are stacked we'll start the long job of scrubbing and sterilizing the tanks for the harvest that's still months away.

That harvest is months away. Its shape is being set right now.

— Evyatar
