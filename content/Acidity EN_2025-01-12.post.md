## TITLE
Bright and Fresh or Mellow Comfort?

## EXCERPT
Is your wine warm and mellow or bright and refreshing? Acidity measures the brightness and sparkle in white and rosé wines---so you can find the perfect match for your meal.

<p>When people describe a wine as "crisp," "zippy," or "refreshing," they're usually talking about <strong>Acidity</strong>. It's what gives a wine its sense of brightness, freshness, and sparkle. A simple example is a fresh salad with and without lemon. The lemon really helps in providing the salad with vibrancy and freshness. The question is how much lemon you like in your salad.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2>The Spectrum of Acidity</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:list -->
<ul><li><strong>Lower Acidity:</strong> These wines feel rounded and gentle on your tongue. They focus on fruit and floral aromas rather than the tart. Smooth, soft, no sharp edges. (Look for <strong>1 or 2</strong> on the scale.)</li><li><em>Best for:</em> Spicy food—high acidity can amplify heat, so a softer wine provides a smooth counterpoint. Also pairs well with delicate dishes that don't need extra "edge."</li><li><strong>Medium Acidity:</strong> These wines find the sweet spot—natural, true, neither too soft nor too sharp. Fresh and clean without being aggressive. (Look for <strong>3</strong> on the scale.)</li><li><em>Best for:</em> A wide variety of everyday meals. These are the all-rounders that work with most dishes.</li><li><strong>Higher Acidity:</strong> These wines have crisp definition and refreshing highlights. They make your mouth water and leave a clean, zesty finish. Vibrant and alive. (Look for <strong>4 or 5</strong> on the scale.)</li><li><em>Best for:</em> Rich, creamy dishes where the brightness cuts through the fat. Also great with fried food, tangy sauces, or anything that benefits from a refreshing contrast.</li></ul>
<!-- /wp:list --></div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3>How to Notice Brightness</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The next time you take a sip of white or rosé, pay attention to the sensation on your tongue:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul><li><strong>Higher Acidity</strong> creates a pleasant tingle, especially on the sides of your mouth. Your mouth waters more. There's a crispness, a sparkle.</li><li><strong>Lower Acidity</strong> feels soft and smooth—no sharpness, just gentle fruit. Rounded and mellow.</li></ul>
<!-- /wp:list --></div>
<!-- /wp:column --></div>

<h3>A Way to Picture It</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Think of it like music—same song, same volume, same number of musicians, but different tonal quality:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul><li><strong>Warm and mellow</strong> — Cello, bass, soft piano. The sound is smooth, rounded with no sharp edges. It wraps around you.</li><li><strong>Balanced and clear</strong> — A mix of warm and bright tones. Natural, true, easy to listen to.</li><li><strong>Bright and sparkling</strong> — Violin, flute, crisp cymbal. The sound has edge, definition, sparkle. It cuts through and wakes you up.</li></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Wine acidity works the same way. It's what makes your mouth water, keeps a wine from tasting "flat," and gives it that refreshing quality.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3>The Acidity Scale</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Each white and rosé shows an <strong>Acidity rating from 1 to 5</strong>:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul><li><strong>Levels 1 &amp; 2:</strong> Warm and mellow. Soft, smooth, gentle.</li><li><strong>Level 3:</strong> Clear and balanced. Fresh without being sharp.</li><li><strong>Levels 4 &amp; 5:</strong> Bright and sparkling. Crisp, zesty, vibrant.</li></ul>
