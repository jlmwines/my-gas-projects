## TITLE
Bright and Fresh or Mellow Comfort?

## EXCERPT
Is your wine warm and mellow or bright and refreshing? Acidity measures the brightness and sparkle in white and rosé wines — so you can find the perfect match for your meal.

<p style="font-size:1.15em">When people describe a wine as "crisp," "zippy," or "refreshing," they're usually talking about <strong>Acidity</strong>. It's what gives a wine its sense of brightness, freshness, and sparkle. A simple example is a fresh salad with and without lemon. The lemon really helps in providing the salad with vibrancy and freshness. The question is how much lemon you like in your salad.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Spectrum of Acidity</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Lower Acidity:</strong> These wines feel rounded and gentle on your tongue. They focus on fruit and floral aromas rather than the tart. Smooth, soft, no sharp edges. (Look for <strong>1 or 2</strong> on the scale.)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><em>Best for:</em> Spicy food — high acidity can amplify heat, so a softer wine provides a smooth counterpoint. Also pairs well with delicate dishes that don't need extra "edge."</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Medium Acidity:</strong> These wines find the sweet spot — natural, true, neither too soft nor too sharp. Fresh and clean without being aggressive. (Look for <strong>3</strong> on the scale.)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><em>Best for:</em> A wide variety of everyday meals. These are the all-rounders that work with most dishes.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Higher Acidity:</strong> These wines have crisp definition and refreshing highlights. They make your mouth water and leave a clean, zesty finish. Vibrant and alive. (Look for <strong>4 or 5</strong> on the scale.)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><em>Best for:</em> Rich, creamy dishes where the brightness cuts through the fat. Also great with fried food, tangy sauces, or anything that benefits from a refreshing contrast.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">How to Notice Brightness</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The next time you take a sip of white or rosé, pay attention to the sensation on your tongue:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Higher Acidity</strong> creates a pleasant tingle, especially on the sides of your mouth. Your mouth waters more. There's a crispness, a sparkle.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Lower Acidity</strong> feels soft and smooth — no sharpness, just gentle fruit. Rounded and mellow.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":67068,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none","className":"mobile-only"} -->

<div class="wp-block-group has-background" style="background-color:#faf6f1;padding:1em"><!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">A Way to Picture It</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Think of it like music — same song, same volume, same number of musicians, but different tonal quality:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Warm and mellow</strong> — Cello, bass, soft piano. Smooth, rounded, it wraps around you.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Balanced and clear</strong> — A mix of warm and bright tones. Natural, easy to listen to.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Bright and sparkling</strong> — Violin, flute, crisp cymbal. Edge, definition, sparkle.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Wine acidity works the same way — it's what keeps a wine from tasting "flat."</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></div>
<!-- /wp:group -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">The Acidity Scale</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Each white and rosé on our site shows an <strong>Acidity rating from 1 to 5</strong>:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Levels 1 &amp; 2:</strong> Warm and mellow. Soft, smooth, gentle.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Level 3:</strong> Clear and balanced. Fresh without being sharp.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Levels 4 &amp; 5:</strong> Bright and sparkling. Crisp, zesty, vibrant.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
