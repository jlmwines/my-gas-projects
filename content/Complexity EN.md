**How Much is Happening in Your Glass?**

Have you ever noticed that some wines showcase one or two flavors while
others seem to unfold into something new with every sip? **Complexity**
describes how many different things are happening in your glass at once.

**The Spectrum of Complexity**

- **Lower Complexity:** These wines have one clear flavor---usually
  fresh fruit like red cherries or crisp green apples. They're
  refreshing, easy-drinking, and perfect when you want something nice
  without overthinking it. The flavor is direct and honest.

- **Medium Complexity:** These wines offer more variety. You'll taste
  the main fruit, but you might also notice something secondary---a
  floral note, a hint of spice, a touch of earthiness. There's more to
  discover in every sip, but it's still approachable.

- **Higher Complexity:** This is a multi-layered experience. You might
  start with deep fruit, but then find additional notes---cedar, toasted
  nuts, vanilla, herbs, or earthy undertones. The sensations shift from
  the moment the wine hits your tongue to the moment you swallow. Every
  sip reveals something new.

**Finding Your Level**

Complexity isn't about quality---it's about the experience you're in the
mood for.

A simple wine is great for a casual afternoon, a weeknight dinner, or
when you just want something refreshing without analysis. A highly
complex wine is for those times you want to slow down and notice how the
flavors unfold and interact---maybe with a special meal or when the wine
itself is the focus.

Neither is "better." It's about matching the experience to your moment.

**A Way to Picture It**

Think of it like music---same song, same volume, but change who's playing:

- **A solo or duet** --- One or two instruments. The melody is clear,
  direct, easy to follow. Nothing competes for your attention.

- **A small ensemble** --- A few more instruments join. You still hear
  the main melody, but now there's harmony, maybe a counterpoint. More
  to notice if you listen.

- **A full orchestra** --- Layers of sound. Strings, brass, woodwinds,
  percussion. The melody is still there, but it's woven into something
  richer. Every time you listen, you might notice something new.

Wine complexity works the same way. It's not about intensity or
brightness---it's about how many flavors are playing at once.

**The Complexity Scale**

Each wine shows a **Complexity rating from 1 to 5**:

- **Levels 1 & 2:** One clear flavor, clean and direct.
- **Level 3:** Main flavor plus harmonies to discover.
- **Levels 4 & 5:** Layers that unfold and evolve.

Whether you want a simple refreshment or a detailed journey, you'll find
the right level.
