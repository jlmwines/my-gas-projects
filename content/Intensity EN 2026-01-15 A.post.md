## TITLE
How Much Presence Does Your Wine Have?

## EXCERPT
How much presence does your wine have? Intensity measures how much attention a red wine asks for — so you can pick the right bottle for every occasion.

<p style="font-size:1.15em">Dry red wines don't all play the same role in your evening. <strong>Intensity</strong> describes how much presence and attention a wine asks for — from a quiet companion in the background to a bold star that demands the spotlight.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column {"width":"55%"} -->
<div class="wp-block-column" style="flex-basis:55%"><!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Spectrum of Intensity</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Lower Intensity:</strong> These reds are pleasant and easy-going. They accompany your meal without interrupting. You might not think much about the wine itself — it's just there, making the evening a little nicer. Perfect when conversation, food, or company is the real focus. There's often beauty and complexity here too, but appreciating it asks you to slow down and notice the details.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Medium Intensity:</strong> These wines have presence. You notice them between bites. They're engaging enough to appreciate, but not so demanding that they take over. A good balance for most evenings.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Higher Intensity:</strong> These wines demand attention. Bold flavors, firm textures, a finish that lingers. Even with food on the table, the wine is a co-star. For evenings when you want the wine itself to be part of the event.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Most people find they settle into a preferred intensity for a while — then it shifts. This is both personal and natural. It also follows the seasons: summer often calls for lighter, fresher experiences, while winter draws us toward deeper, more comforting flavors.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></div>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Choosing the Right Role</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Matching the intensity to your occasion is the secret to a great wine experience:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>The Casual Evening:</strong> A lighter red lets conversation flow. It's there, it's good, but it's not asking you to stop and analyze it. <em>Try with:</em> Light dishes, simple meals, or when the company is the main event.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>The Everyday Dinner:</strong> A medium-intensity red complements your food without competing. Enough presence to enjoy, easy enough to engage with but doesn't take over. <em>Try with:</em> Pasta, roast chicken, grilled vegetables — most weeknight meals.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>The Wine-Forward Occasion:</strong> A bold red is for when you want to pay attention. Maybe it's a special bottle, a celebratory meal, or you just want to slow down and taste. <em>Try with:</em> Rich dishes that can stand up to it — or on its own, when the wine is the point.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">A Way to Picture It</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Think of it like music — specifically, the volume at which it's played. Every wine comes with its own volume setting:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Quiet background music</strong> — An intimate space, conversation taking the lead. The wine is there, but it's not asking for attention.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Comfortable listening level</strong> — You enjoy the music between conversations. It adds to the evening without dominating.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Concert volume</strong> — The music fills the room. You're paying attention, and the wine is part of the event.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>You don't control the dial — the wine does. Your job is to match the setting to the volume.</p>
