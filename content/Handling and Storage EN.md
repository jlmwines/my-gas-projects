A great bottle deserves a little care, and none of it is complicated. Start with storage: wine doesn't like heat, light, or vibration, so skip the kitchen counter and pick an interior cabinet that stays cool year-round. When you open it, twist the opener, not the bottle, and lift the cork out gently. Temperature is where most of us slip up. A red served too warm tastes boozy; a white too cold hides its character. Serve reds slightly cooler than room temperature (fifteen minutes in the fridge if your home runs warm) and whites straight from the fridge. Pour to the widest part of the glass, about a third full, so the wine has room to open up. And if you don't finish the bottle, don't pour it away: a vacuum stopper and the fridge keep it fresh for days, reds included. Just let them warm a little before the next pour. Small habits, better wine.

**Getting the Best from Every Bottle**

Wine is a living, delicate creation. To make sure you experience the full "orchestra" of intensity and all the layers of complexity we've talked about, a little bit of care goes a long way. This guide covers the basics—from the moment your box arrives to the final sip.

**Storage: Finding the "Coolest Spot"**

Wine doesn't like light, heat, or vibration. You don't need a fancy cellar, but you do need to avoid the kitchen counter or anywhere near a window.

- **The Goal:** A cool, dark place with a steady temperature.
- **The JLM Tip:** Look for an interior cabinet or a closet far away from doors and appliances. If it feels cool to your touch year-round, it's a good home for your wine.

**Opening: Finesse Over Force**

Opening a bottle is an exciting moment. To keep the cork intact:

1. Use the blade of your opener to cut the foil cap cleanly.
2. Center your opener and twist it into the cork.
3. **The Secret:** Twist the opener, not the bottle. Gently lift the cork out to avoid any breakage.

**Decanting: When It Helps**

Some wines benefit from a little air before serving. Pouring the wine into a decanter or glass pitcher exposes it to oxygen, which can help release aromas and soften the texture.

Most wines we sell are ready to enjoy straight from the bottle. When decanting is recommended, we note it on the product page and your packing slip. If you see the suggestion:

- **Young Reds:** Give them **30–60 minutes**. This softens the wine and brings the fruit flavors forward.
- **Mature Reds:** These might need **1–2 hours** to wake up the subtle notes that have developed over years of aging.

No decanting note? Just pour and enjoy.

**Serving: Temperature & Glassware**

Temperature is the most common mistake people make. If a wine is too warm, it tastes boozy; if it's too cold, you can't taste the complexity.

- **Reds:** Serve **slightly cooler than room temperature**. If your house is warm, put the red in the fridge for 15 minutes before opening.
- **Whites:** Serve **chilled**.
- **Sparkling:** Serve **very chilled**.

**The Glass:** You don't need expensive crystals. A simple glass with a tapered bowl works best.

- **The 1/3 Rule:** Pour your wine to the widest point of the bowl (about one-third full). This gives the wine room to swirl and release its scents.
- **The Stem:** Hold the glass by the stem. This keeps your hands from warming up the wine (unless it's a bit too cold, in which case, cupping the bowl is perfectly fine!).

**Saving Some for Later**

Once opened, wine begins to change. If you don't finish the bottle, you have two main tools:

1. **Vacuum Pumps:** These remove the air from the bottle and create a seal, slowing down the fading of the flavor.
2. **Refrigeration:** Always put opened wine (even red!) in the fridge. It acts like a "pause button" for the wine's development. Just remember to let the reds warm up slightly before you drink them the next day.

**Final Thoughts**

At JLM Wines, we want every bottle to be a delightful experience. These simple steps ensure that the wine you enjoy at home is exactly what we intended when we selected it for our collection.
