<p style="font-size:1.15em">Many people feel they need a degree in culinary arts to pair wine with food. It really should be as simple as choosing a side dish. One rule matters most - if you enjoy or are in the mood for a very specific type of wine - that is the wine that will best accompany your meal.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Forget the technical rules. Here are two simple ways to think about your glass and your plate.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Secret 1: Choose Your Pairing Style</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Instead of memorizing flavor profiles, just ask yourself how you want the tastes to interact:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Harmonizing:</strong> The wine and food share the same energy. A crisp, bright white with grilled fish—both are light and refreshing. They complement each other naturally, each reinforcing the other.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Contrasting:</strong> The wine does what the food cannot. A bold red with a rich, fatty steak—the wine's presence cuts through the richness, refreshing your palate between bites. Both the food and wine are enhanced by the other.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list --></div>

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Secret 2: Know Your Food Type</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Almost every dish fits into one of four groups. Once you know which one you're serving, the wine choice becomes clear.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":67055,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="https://staging6.jlmwines.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/pairing-mild.jpg" alt="Delicate white wine beside light fish — mild food pairing" class="wp-image-67055"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Mild</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Soft cheeses, white fish, lightly seasoned poultry, or pasta in a light sauce.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>What works:</em> A fresh white wine with crisp acidity and delicate fruit. The wine harmonizes with gentle flavors without overwhelming them.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":67043,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="https://staging6.jlmwines.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/pairing-cheese.jpg" alt="Wine glass beside aged cheese and olives — rich food pairing" class="wp-image-67043"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Rich</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Salmon, red meats, fattier foods, hard cheeses, or anything with butter and cream.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>What works:</em> A medium-intensity red with rich fruit. Or a white with higher acidity to cut through the richness—the brightness cleanses your palate between bites.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":67054,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="https://staging6.jlmwines.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/pairing-intense.jpg" alt="Deep red wine beside spiced dish — intense food pairing" class="wp-image-67054"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Intense</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Spicy dishes, heavy seasonings, bold garlic, or strong flavors that announce themselves.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>What works:</em> A complex red with bold fruit. For spicy heat, choose <strong>lower acidity</strong>—high-acid wines amplify the burn, while softer wines cool it down.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":67053,"sizeSlug":"large","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="https://staging6.jlmwines.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/pairing-sweet.jpg" alt="Rosé wine beside a golden pastry — sweet food pairing" class="wp-image-67053"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Sweet</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Desserts, pastries, or savory dishes with sweet glazes, sauces, or fruit.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>What works:</em> A wine with fruity sweetness of its own, or a gentle dry wine. The sweetness harmonizes rather than clashes—intense dry wines may taste bitter against sweet food.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></div>
<!-- /wp:column --></div>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Using the Scales</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Each wine on our site shows simple 1-5 scales. Here's how to use them:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">For Red Wines (Intensity &amp; Complexity)</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Rich foods</strong> love higher <strong>Intensity</strong>—a bold dish needs a wine that won't fade into the background.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Intense dishes</strong> pair well with higher <strong>Complexity</strong>—more layers in the wine means more flavors to engage with the dish's boldness.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list --></div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">For White &amp; Rosé Wines (Complexity &amp; Acidity)</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Rich or creamy dishes</strong> pair with higher <strong>Acidity</strong>—brightness cuts through fat and keeps the meal feeling fresh.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Spicy foods</strong> need <strong>lower Acidity</strong>—high-acid wines amplify heat, while softer wines provide relief.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list --></div>
