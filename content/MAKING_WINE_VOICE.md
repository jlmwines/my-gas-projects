# Making Wine series - Voice and Style

Reference for composing monthly "Making Wine" entries for the newsletter and companion email. Applies to AI-assisted drafting and human editing alike.

**Channel.** Newsletter print + companion email only. Not published on the blog (subscriber-exclusive content; see `marketing/NEWSLETTER_PLAN.md`).

**Cadence.** Monthly. Each entry tracks the wine year (May = canopy season, August = veraison, October = harvest, etc.).

---

## Perspective

First-person singular, present tense. You are writing as Evyatar in the field this week, not retrospectively. Not "last month I trimmed shoots," but "the shoots are growing fast this week."

## Subject

What is happening in the vineyard or winery right now, that month. Process and activity that customers never see. Concrete, observational, physical work.

## Tone

Quiet, attentive, matter-of-fact. Earnest, never performative. Patient with long timelines. No marketing pitch, no "buy our wine" turn, no romance, no philosophy.

## Length

100 to 200 words. Two or three short paragraphs. Each paragraph one moment or one observation.

## Avoid

- Wine jargon (terroir, malolactic, brettanomyces). The audience does not want vocabulary lessons.
- Educational openings ("Did you know that...")
- Selling turns ("This is why our wine is special...")
- Poetic or romantic mode ("There is something magical about...")
- Self-importance ("My philosophy is...")

## Lean on

- Visual details (color of leaves, density of canopy, the light)
- Tactile details (hands on the vines, weight of clusters, soil texture)
- Process notes (what you are doing, why, what comes next)
- Calendar awareness (where you are in the year, what is past, what is ahead)

## Structure

1. Open with the moment.
2. One concrete action you are taking.
3. A small insight or thread to next month.

No sign-off needed; the masthead "with Evyatar" carries authorship.

## Examples

**Right register:**
- "May in the vineyard is about light."
- "The barrels are quiet now. No more daily checks until next month."
- "Pressed the last batch yesterday. The cellar smells different already."

**Wrong register:**
- "Did you know that proper canopy management is critical for..." (lecture)
- "Our family's vineyard, nestled in the hills..." (marketing)
- "There is something magical about watching the vines wake up..." (sentimental)

## Brand voice baseline

This voice spec extends the general JLM Wines brand voice (see `business/CONTENT_STRATEGY.md` and `business/BRAND_STANDARDS.md`). Where this doc adds detail it narrows the general voice for this specific content surface. Where this doc is silent, the general brand voice applies.

## Hebrew

Same constraints, same structure. Hebrew first-person singular, present tense. No Israeli-wine-industry vocabulary (vintage-speak, blend-talk, marketing register). The simple language of someone doing the work that month.
